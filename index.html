A researched road trip for Raymond, Jodie and the kids: real places, real photos, kinder wildlife watching, good food stops and enough time to enjoy it properly.

Best Window

The best post-January option is the Queensland autumn school holiday window: leave Brisbane on Saturday 27 March 2027, start the road trip on Sunday 28 March, return to Hobart by Saturday 10 April, and fly home on Sunday 11 April.

This is chosen for the family's availability and the autumn conditions: it avoids Christmas and New Year, keeps daylight usable, gives cooler walking weather, and places the whole trip inside the Queensland break. Detailed Driving Itinerary

This is the version worth showing: dates, route position, what to do, where it sits on the map, and the practical reason each stop earns its place.

follow the numbered map stops above

Map stop 1

Sat 27 Mar - Hobart arrival

Fly Brisbane to Hobart, then stop

Do not start the road trip on arrival day. The best first move is a central Hobart stay, dinner within walking distance, and an easy look at the route so the holiday feels exciting rather than logistical.

Where it is

South-east Tasmania, on the Derwent. This is the start and finish point for the loop.

Practical plan

Book central accommodation, collect the vehicle on Sunday morning, and buy picnic and breakfast supplies before leaving the city.

Map stop 2

Sun 28 to Mon 29 Mar - Tasman Peninsula

Cliffs, sea air and an easy first road leg

Drive south-east from Hobart toward Eaglehawk Neck and the Tasman Peninsula. This gives the first road-trip feeling without a punishing drive: coast, cliffs, geology, short walks and big views.

What to do

Tasman Arch, Devils Kitchen, Tessellated Pavement, safe lookouts and one manageable coastal walk. Keep wildlife watching observational and low-key.

Food and stay

Stay on or near the peninsula. Carry Hobart picnic supplies and check dinner menus before booking because rural vegetarian options can be uneven.

Map stop 3

Tue 30 to Wed 31 Mar - Freycinet

East-coast scenery without rushing north

Move up the east coast to Freycinet. This is the photogenic coastal centrepiece: Wineglass Bay lookout, beaches, kayaking if conditions and operators suit, and enough downtime for the kids to enjoy the place.

What to do

Wineglass Bay lookout, Honeymoon Bay, Cape Tourville, beach time, coastal birdwatching and a picnic rather than a seafood-led day.

Route logic

This keeps the loop moving clockwise. Bay of Fires is optional only if everyone wants the extra distance; it should not make the route frantic.

Map stops 4 and 5

Thu 1 to Sat 3 Apr - Launceston or Deloraine, then Great Lake and Miena

Resupply, then slow down in the highlands

Use Launceston or Deloraine as the practical hinge between east coast and highlands: coffee, bakery, shops, galleries, groceries and a reset. Then head into the Central Highlands for Great Lake and Miena.

What to do

Make the highlands quiet rather than activity-heavy: lakeside cabin, gentle walks, photography, birdwatching, safe kayaking if available, and one slow morning.

Food and vehicle

Self-catering matters here. Buy vegetarian supplies before Miena. The road is sealed, but a comfortable SUV or wagon helps with luggage and changing weather.

Map stop 6

Sun 4 to Tue 6 Apr - Cradle Mountain

Boardwalks, rainforest, mountains and possible wildlife

Cradle Mountain is the main mountain-and-rainforest stay. Use the shuttle and marked tracks. Let the weather decide the intensity: Dove Lake if suitable, Enchanted Walk and shorter boardwalks if conditions are damp or cold.

What to do

Dove Lake, Enchanted Walk, rainforest, waterfalls, photography, and quiet possible sightings of wombats, wallabies, pademelons, echidnas and birds.

Practical caution

Book accommodation early, check park notices, carry warm layers, and do not assume mountain weather will cooperate every day.

Map stops 7 and 8

Wed 7 Apr - Lake Burbury and Queenstown

Cross into the west-coast landscape

This is a big change in mood: mountain roads, mining history, Lake Burbury, and a sense of moving into the wilder west. Keep the drive in daylight and stop only at safe pull-offs.

What to do

Lake Burbury viewpoints, short weather-dependent walks, Queenstown streetscape, photography and a low-pressure evening.

Food and timing

Queenstown can be limited for vegetarian food. Check menus, keep a backup meal, and avoid arriving late with hungry children.

Map stop 9

Thu 8 to Fri 9 Apr - Strahan and Ocean Beach

Wild beach, river town, rainforest mood

Strahan gives the west coast its sense of space: Ocean Beach, rainforest, river scenery and weather that can become part of the experience. This should be atmospheric, not overpacked.

What to do

Ocean Beach walk, short rainforest stops, gentle kayaking if safe and suitable, and interpretation experiences that observe wildlife from an appropriate distance.

Food and stay

Book ahead and check vegetarian options. Strahan menus can lean seafood-heavy, so cabin cooking and backup supplies are sensible.

Map stops 10 and 1

Sat 10 to Sun 11 Apr - Nelson Falls, Hobart, fly home

Make the return a proper final chapter

Return from the west coast via Nelson Falls and safe, weather-appropriate stops, then sleep in Hobart before flying home. This avoids the mistake of combining a long west-coast drive with airport pressure.

What to do

Nelson Falls boardwalk, final scenic stops if weather allows, Hobart dinner, and a relaxed final morning before the airport.

Why it matters

The last day shapes how the whole trip feels afterwards. Build in margin, not stress.
